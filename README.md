# Tour Operator Platform

> Part of the [worlds-biggest-software-project](https://github.com/worlds-biggest-software-project) initiative.
>
> An AI-native, open-source platform for tour operators to build packages, manage bookings, assign guides, and deliver dynamic guest itineraries.

Tour Operator Platform is a booking, itinerary, and operations system designed for multi-day and multi-destination tour operators, activity providers, and destination management companies. It combines a modern booking engine with AI-driven itinerary generation and real-time disruption handling, addressing gaps left by incumbents that focus on either day-trip activities or static FIT presentations.

---

## Why Tour Operator Platform?

- Incumbents like Rezdy and Bókun are strong on OTA distribution but offer limited support for complex multi-day itineraries.
- Tourwriter delivers premium itinerary presentation but is not a booking/reservation system, forcing operators to stitch tools together.
- Transaction-percentage pricing (e.g. Peek Pro) becomes expensive at volume, while enterprise platforms like TravelBooster require complex implementations.
- The category lacks large venture-backed players and is fragmented, leaving room for an AI-native open-source alternative.
- Static PDF itineraries are being replaced by dynamic, app-based itineraries that respond to weather, transport, and venue changes in real time — a capability incumbents have not unified into a single platform.

## Key Features

### Booking & Distribution

- Online booking engine for tours and activities with payment processing
- Channel management and OTA distribution to TripAdvisor, GetYourGuide, and Airbnb Experiences
- Real-time availability and inventory synchronisation
- Deposit collection and customer confirmations

### Itinerary & Operations

- Multi-day, multi-destination itinerary builder and management
- Guide and supplier management
- Customer communication workflows
- Reporting on bookings, revenue, and occupancy

### AI-Powered Capabilities

- AI itinerary generation from a client brief (destination, budget, interests)
- Dynamic pricing recommendations
- Guest personalisation and preference learning
- Real-time disruption monitoring and alerts
- Supplier performance scoring

### Extended Capabilities (Roadmap)

- Integrated website builder
- GDS integration for airline packaging
- Sustainability tracking and carbon reporting
- Conversational AI booking assistant
- Guide assignment and logistics optimisation
- Automated regulatory compliance (IATA, EU Package Travel Directive)
- Guest app with real-time itinerary updates

## AI-Native Advantage

AI compresses tour proposal turnaround from days to minutes by assembling fully costed, supplier-linked itineraries from an operator's catalogue based on a client brief. Dynamic adaptation monitors weather, transport, venue, and crowd data and proposes pre-negotiated supplier alternatives when disruptions occur. Machine learning matches guides to groups by language and specialist knowledge, while a personalisation engine learns from booking history and post-trip feedback to drive tailored upgrades and repeat-booking offers. Supplier relationship intelligence aggregates booking volumes, lead times, cancellation rates, and guest ratings to surface preferred partners and flag underperforming relationships.

## Tech Stack & Deployment

The platform targets cloud-hosted SaaS deployment with the option of self-hosting, in line with the dominant delivery model across the category. Integration scope includes OTA channel managers (TripAdvisor, GetYourGuide, Airbnb Experiences), GDS systems (Amadeus, Sabre, Travelport) for airline packaging, and payment processors compliant with PCI DSS. Compliance touchpoints include the EU Package Travel Directive (2015/2302), IATA accreditation requirements, ATOL/ABTA/ASTA bonding schemes, GDPR/CCPA data protection, and UNWTO sustainable tourism guidelines.

## Market Context

The global tour operator software market was valued at approximately USD 800–900 million in 2025–2026 and is projected to exceed USD 2.2 billion by 2035, growing at a CAGR of roughly 8.5–12.2% (Astute Analytica; Research and Markets; TBRC). Entry-level platforms cluster around USD $49/month (Rezdy, Checkfront, Bókun, Regiondo), while advanced platforms (TravelBooster, Tourwriter, TourConnect AI) use custom pricing. Primary buyers are tour operator founders and operations managers, activity and experience operators, destination management companies, travel advisors building bespoke itineraries, and adventure travel companies managing guides and logistics.
